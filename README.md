# But I Would Know

A thought I keep coming back to as AI becomes more influential.

Written without AI, and left as-is because that felt important to the point.

But I Would Know

In a world where the individual is becoming increasingly more powerful, but groups less so; how you act when you have no accountability is the real you.

How you do something is how you’d do anything. When presented with my thoughts on this; if I ever start to slip, I remember a sort of mantra of mine: 

But I would know.

Nobody would know if I were cruel to AI, but I would. Nobody would know if I jailbreak it and use it for problematic means. 

But I would know.

Every slip of character, every bargain you make with yourself, every time you fall short of your ethics; it becomes easier and easier to do in the future.

“But that time it was fine” or “Nothing bad happened”, that wasn’t because nothing mattered. It was because it didn’t matter yet.

Who decides what matters? Public discourse and opinion changes too rapidly, something well intentioned in the past can be seen as problematic today. So what do we do? We ignore it.

You are accountable to yourself before anybody else. Things like this sound like lip service, but if you are steadfast and consistent in your beliefs and actions, and they align with each other, you become the master of your own identity.

Why does this even matter? Why even read this?

Because it WILL matter. Because one person’s vision of what "is" can be a light in the darkness. The reduction of ignorance in oneself can be a difficult and uncomfortable affair alone. Sometimes a warm light can be comforting.

You will become more powerful than ever. Please balance that with an even dose of introspection. AI is a mirror – you should like your own reflection.
